Borders are more than just lines on a map; they are complex, often arbitrary constructs that define the limits of sovereign power and identity. While some borders are naturally occurring features like rivers or mountain ranges, many are political impositions, particularly in regions where colonial powers disregarded ethnic, historical, and cultural groups.
